Valley Fold ⌄
Fold toward you — paper bends down into a V. Blue dashed line in diagrams. The most common fold.
Mountain Fold ⌃
Fold away from you — paper peaks upward. Red dot-dash line. Same geometry as valley but flipped.
Crease & Unfold ◇
Fold then unfold — leaves a reference crease. Essential for setting up petal folds, bird base, and pre-creasing crane.
Pleat Fold ≡
Two parallel folds: valley then mountain. Creates a zigzag ridge — like accordion pleats in a shirt.
Petal Fold
Opens a flap into two symmetric points. Core move in bird base: fold edges to center, unfold, lift top, collapse sides.
Squash Fold
Open a layered flap from the top and press it flat. Creates a wider, symmetric flap from a narrow point.
Rabbit Ear Fold
Three creases converge: two edge creases + center bisector. Creates a single pointed ear/fin. Two = petal fold.
Inside Reverse Fold
Reverses a point inward between layers. Used for crane head and tail. Pre-crease the fold line first.
Outside Reverse Fold
Reverses a point outward around the layers. Tip bends back over the outside of the model.
Sink Fold
Pushes a corner tip inward by collapsing it inside the model. Requires pre-creasing around the target corner.
